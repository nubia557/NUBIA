VERVAL VIOLENCE
Verbal violence is a form of psychological aggression that is manifested through words, phrases or expressions with the intention of emotionally harming another person. It is often used in situations of conflict or control, and its effects can be as damaging as other forms of violence, although sometimes it is less visible. This violence does not leave physical marks, but its consequences on the mental and emotional health of the victim can be devastating.
TYPES OF VERVAL VIOLENCE

Insults: Words or phrases are used that denigrate or humiliate the other person, such as calling someone offensive or insulting names.

Disqualification: Consists of belittling the person's abilities, achievements or characteristics, making them feel inferior or incapable.

Destructive criticism: Unlike constructive criticism, which seeks to improve, destructive criticism directly attacks the person, with no intention of improving their behavior, but rather of harming them.

Yelling: Using a loud, aggressive tone of voice can be very intimidating and emotionally affect the victim.

Emotional manipulation: Words are used to manipulate the other person's emotions, generating guilt, fear or confusion to control them.
CONSEQUENCES

Damage to self-esteem: People who are constantly verbally attacked may begin to doubt their own worth, which affects their confidence and self-esteem.

Stress and anxiety: Constant exposure to hurtful words can generate a state of anxiety, tension and stress.

Depression: Victims of verbal violence often experience symptoms of depression, as they feel overwhelmed and isolated by the emotional damage.

Emotional disorders: Emotional disorders such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) may arise, especially if verbal violence continues for a considerable time.

Impact on relationships: Verbal violence can affect interpersonal relationships, generating distrust and distancing between the people involved.

 
FACTORS

Power inequality: In many cases, verbal violence is used as a tool to maintain control and superiority over the other person, which can occur in work, family or couple relationships.

Stress and frustration: People who go through stressful or frustrating situations sometimes vent their emotions through hurtful words toward others.

Lack of communication skills: People who do not know how to express their emotions in a healthy way may resort to verbal violence as a means of communication.

Learned behavior patterns: People who have grown up in an environment where verbal violence was common can replicate this behavior in their own relationships.
PREVENTION AND SOLUTION

  • Emotional education: Encouraging emotional education from an early age can help people manage their emotions effectively, avoiding resorting to verbal violence.
  • Promote empathy: Helping people put themselves in each other's shoes can reduce the use of hurtful words and encourage more respectful communication.
  • Assertive communication: Teaching assertive communication allows you to express needs and concerns in a respectful and effective way, without resorting to verbal violence.
  • Professional intervention: In serious cases, psychological therapy can be useful for both the victim and the aggressor. Helps understand the effects of verbal violence and improve communication and conflict resolution skills.
  • Helplines: In situations of persistent verbal violence, seeking support from helplines or psychological care centers can be a crucial step in recovery.
VERVAL VIOLENCE IN DIFFERENT CONTEXTS

Verbal violence in the couple: In relationships, verbal violence can be a form of control and manipulation, deeply affecting the emotional health of the victim.

Verbal violence in the workplace: In work environments, verbal harassment can have serious effects on the well-being of employees, decreasing their productivity and motivation.

Verbal violence in the family: Within the family environment, especially between parents and children, verbal violence can have devastating effects on the emotional development of children, affecting their self-esteem and future relationships.

Verbal violence on social networks: Online platforms have become a space where verbal violence can proliferate, through comments, insults and attacks towards other people, especially affecting young people.
CONCLUSION

Verbal violence is a form of emotional abuse that, although it does not leave visible scars, can be equally or more harmful than other forms of violence. It is essential to approach this type of violence seriously, educating people about the importance of respectful and empathetic communication. Early intervention and psychological support are key to preventing and treating the effects of verbal violence, thus guaranteeing healthier and more harmonious relationships.
